Accommodations

Mission

Preparing Leaders of Tomorrow

PH International’s Youth Ambassadors Africa Program (YA-AF) strengthens mutual understanding, prosperity, and global collaboration by empowering youth leaders to become active, responsible citizens both locally and globally.

The program fosters mutual respect, helping to share values, build relationships, and bridge cultural divides to promote peaceful coexistence. As these leaders return home, they contribute to global stability, creating international partnerships that benefit mutual interests and enhance security, economic growth, and progress.

The Exchange Experience

The Youth Ambassadors Program brings together high school students and adult mentors from countries across the globe to promote mutual understanding, increase leadership skills, and prepare youth to make a difference in their communities. While exchanges engage heavily with Latin America and the Caribbean, they also include highly selective delegations from Africa and the United States.

The program builds team spirit through hands-on service learning, leadership seminars, peer collaboration, and visits to entrepreneurial ventures and innovative local organizations.

A balanced mix of homestays, dorms, and hotels offers participants varied perspectives on American life. Homestays provide firsthand exposure to U.S. values, hospitality, and community life through daily interactions with American families. Dorm stays not only foster peer connections but also offer a window into campus life and American higher education. Hotel accommodations support large group gatherings and events with local leaders—and they also offer valuable downtime for participants to bond, reflect, and relax together as a cohort.

Impact & Outcomes

The themes of civic education and youth leadership build participants’ self-confidence, resilience, and capacity to drive solutions to local challenges in their communities. Adult mentors will strengthen their mentoring and facilitation skills, enhancing support for youth-led initiatives.

Upon their return home, the students apply what they have learned to implement projects that serve critical needs in their communities, ensuring long-term alignment with international values and local progress.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the primary goal of the program?

To promote mutual understanding, increase leadership skills, and prepare youth to make a tangible difference in their home communities upon their return.

What does the daily itinerary look like?

Participants engage in civic workshops, community service activities, team-building exercises, and formal meetings with local U.S. community leaders.

What happens after the exchange?

Students are required to apply what they have learned to design and implement formal community service projects that address specific needs within their home districts.
