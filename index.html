Official Statement

A Commitment to Serve

Public service is not about titles, recognition, or position. It is about responsibility. It is about choosing to care when it would be easier to stay silent, choosing to act when others wait, and choosing to serve because the future of our country depends on the commitment of its people.

As a young Seychellois, I believe that leadership begins with service. Our generation has a duty to contribute, to question, to build, and to help shape a Seychelles where every young person feels seen, heard, and empowered to take part in national progress.

The Office of Tyler Nicholas exists to support that mission — to create a space for civic leadership, youth advocacy, community action, and meaningful public service.

“Ask not what your country can do for you — ask what you can do for your country.” — President John F. Kennedy

That message remains powerful today. It reminds us that progress does not begin with waiting for change. It begins when we decide to become part of it.

This Office is my commitment to serve, to listen, and to work for a stronger, more united, and more hopeful Seychelles.

Tyler P. Nicholas
